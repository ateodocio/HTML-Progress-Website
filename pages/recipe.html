Simple Bread Pudding

>> Leftover stale bread? No problem. I got your back homie.
This incredibly simple (pun intended) recipe will have your mouth watering.
All you need is about 5-10 minutes of prep time and you can leave the rest of the job for your oven!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup of sugar
  • pinch of salt
  • 4-6 loaves of bread cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 2 eggs
  • your choice of topping (fruits, chocolate chips, or assorted nuts)

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees. In a small saucepan over low heat, warm milk, butter, vanilla, sugar and salt. Continue cooking just until butter melts; cool. Meanwhile, butter a 4-to-6-cup baking dish and fill it with cubed bread.
  2. Add eggs to cooled milk mixture and whisk; pour mixture over bread. Let bread sit in the mixture for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  3. Bake for 30 to 45 minutes, or until custard is set but still a little wobbly and edges of bread have browned. Serve warm or at room temperature with your topping of choice!.


Nutella Mug Cake

Sometimes we're impatient and that impatient births great recipes!
If you have a microwave and a cup, then you'll love this one. It takes only a minute or two in cooking time.
This is super convenient when you're craving for a good brownie-cake dessert.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup of whole milk
  • 1/4 cup of flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 cup of Nutella

Steps

  1. Combine milk, flour, and baking power in a 12-ounce microwave-safe mug. Whisk until smooth.
  2. Add the Nutella and stir until the batter has little to no clumps. Honestly, I just use a fork to mix.
  3. Microwave on high for about 45 to 60 seconds. Add 15 more seconds depending on the brownie-like consistency you want.
  4. For best results, serve with vanila ice cream on top. Yum!
